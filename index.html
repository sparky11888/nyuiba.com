Every spring club members have an opportunity to run for a position on the IBA executive board. Project Management Committee

The Project Management Committee is a select group of 5 or so students that will overlook a series of projects for IBA over the Spring semester. They will be representatives as well as have specific roles within the committee. The PMC will also be responsible for creating IBA's 2015 ISP guide as well as organizing events. Being a part of our PMC is a great way to not only substantiate yourself within IBA but also to be at the heart of the association; PMC members are expected to join all weekly e-board meetings and have a higher chance of being elected into IBA's e-board.

What We Do

The International Business Association (IBA) is a pre-professional student run organization at New York University's Leonard N. Stern School of Business. Our mission is to promote global thinking amongst the Stern student body by aligning our activities with the international study components of NYU Stern's undergraduate curriculum. We also work to increase awareness of global affairs and to foster discussion and exchange of ideas amongst Stern students.

Our professional program promotes the value of international careers, and tries to create a better understanding of business through events with experts from diverse fields. We....

    • Align our strategy and activities with NYU Stern's international curriculum.
    • Support NYU Study Abroad, IBEX, and the IB co-major through informative events that disseminate information and promote the value of these programs.
    • Enhance overseas Stern communities through the Ambassadors initiative.
    • Increase the value of ISP by creating yearly student-guides to the destination cities.
    • Disseminate global thinking and internationalism at NYU Stern.
    • Increase awareness and interest in global affairs through the Round Table.
    • Enhance overseas Stern communities through the Ambassadors initiative.
    • Foster the exchange of ideas and opinions amongst students by broadening the Round Table's community of writers and readers.
    • Promote the value of diversity and multiculturalism in business and outside it.
    • Provide the most diverse and global professional program at NYU Stern.
    • Promote overseas internship and full-time career opportunities.
    • Attract speakers from various fields including government, non-profit and private.
    • Increase awareness on the power of business in making a positive social impact.
    • Create events that highlight the importance of business skills in the public sector.

Come out To Our Biggest Events of the Year
